How to Create an Effective Resume?


A resume, also known as a curriculum vitae (CV) in some regions, is a document that provides a summary of a person's education, work experience, skills, achievements, and qualifications. It is typically used when applying for jobs and serves as a marketing tool to showcase an individual's background and suitability for a specific position.


A good resume is important because it serves as your first impression when applying for a job. In a simple way, it's like a snapshot of your skills, qualifications, and experiences that can convince employers to consider you as a potential candidate. It highlights your relevant achievements and demonstrates why you are the right fit for the position. A well-crafted resume can increase your chances of getting noticed by employers, securing job interviews, and ultimately landing your desired job.


A good resume is important because it serves as your personal marketing tool when applying for a job. It is typically the first impression that potential employers have of you, and it can significantly impact your chances of getting an interview. Here's a simple example to illustrate the importance of a good resume:

Imagine you are an employer with a limited amount of time to review numerous resumes for a single job opening. You receive two resumes that are quite similar in terms of qualifications and experience, but one is well-organized, visually appealing, and effectively highlights the applicant's relevant skills and achievements. The other resume, however, is cluttered, poorly formatted, and lacks clear structure.

Given the time constraints, which resume do you think the employer will be more inclined to spend time reading and consider for an interview? The well-presented resume is likely to grab the employer's attention and make a positive impression, increasing the chances of the applicant being invited for an interview.

This example highlights how a good resume can help you stand out from other candidates and make a strong initial impression on potential employers. It showcases your qualifications, skills, and accomplishments in a clear and concise manner, making it easier for employers to assess your suitability for the job. Ultimately, a well-crafted resume increases your chances of advancing to the next stage of the hiring process and ultimately landing the job you desire.


  • The format and structure of a professional resume is important. The content is what gets you the job, but an aesthetically pleasing resume shows that you took the time to get it right, and that you have the basic computer skills to make it look good. Here are some formatting tips when it comes to writing a resume:
  • * Content should be left-aligned
  • * Margins should be at one inch
  • * Use an easy-to-read, professional font.
  • * Clearly divide each section
  • * Use clear headers for every section
  • * Use bullet points whenever possible for conciseness
  • * Try to keep your resume to one page

There are many professional fonts you can use, including Cambria, Times New Roman, Calibri, Georgia, Arial, Lato and Didot. Your name and header sections should be larger, but keep the basic font size between 10 and 12 points for normal text. Headers and section titles should be between 14-16 points.

Between text, use 1.0 or 1.15 line spacing, and adjust that to double lines after any subheadings. You can increase or decrease if your resume is too full or too empty.

2. Combination Resume:

As the name suggests, a combination resume combines elements of both the chronological and functional formats. It highlights both your work history and skills. It begins with a skills summary or qualifications section followed by a chronological listing of your work experience.

3. Targeted Resume:

A targeted resume is tailored to a specific job or industry. It highlights the skills and experiences that are most relevant to the position you are applying for. You customize your resume for each application to match the requirements of the job posting.

4. Online/Electronic Resume

With the rise of digital platforms, online resumes are becoming more popular. These resumes are specifically designed for online applications and may include hyperlinks to your portfolio, LinkedIn profile, or other relevant websites. They can be easily shared and accessed online.
